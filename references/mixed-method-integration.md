# Mixed-Method Integration

Use this when both survey-style quantitative data and qualitative interview or open-text data are present.

## Hard sequencing rule

Do not integrate too early.

Required order:
1. linkage and comparability check
2. quantitative analysis complete
3. qualitative analysis complete
4. mixed-method integration
5. insight and report writing

If a prior stage is incomplete, integration is not ready.

## Step 0: Linkage first when possible

If the two sources can be linked:
- confirm the linkage field
- use raw source data, not already-deduplicated outputs, for linkage
- enrich interview records with decision-relevant survey attributes
- report match rate, unmatched cases, and duplicate-linked cases

If they cannot be linked:
- state that clearly
- compare sample composition instead
- lower confidence on person-level interpretations

## Step 1: Sample comparability and bias

Before synthesis, check:
- whether interview participants differ materially from the survey base
- whether linked cases are representative or skewed
- whether sample differences constrain interpretation

This must appear in the final limitations section if the bias is meaningful.

## Step 2: Single-source completion

Before integration, each side should have stable outputs:
- quant: core findings, relevant cross-tabs, tests if needed, charts/tables, limitations
- qual: codebook, patterning, casebook, contradiction notes, quote bank, limitations

## Step 3: Integration lenses

For each major integrated finding, classify the relationship:
- Agreement: both sources point in the same direction
- Tension: they appear to disagree
- Complementarity: one source explains or deepens the other

## Step 4: Account versus explanation

Keep separate:
- account: what users say explains their behavior
- explanation: what the combined evidence suggests may actually drive the behavior

Strong reports do not treat self-explanations as automatically sufficient.

## Step 5: Variation and proposition development

For important findings, ask:
- Which users fit the pattern most strongly?
- Which users break the pattern?
- Under what conditions does the pattern weaken?
- What candidate proposition follows from the evidence?

Propositions should be testable and bounded.

## Required outputs

- integration memo
- agreement / tension / complementarity table
- linked-sample bias note
- proposition list
- integrated findings for report use
